### What Is SMGuitar?

### The Problem We Solve

Learning music theory is hard. Really hard.

Traditional methods involve reading dense textbooks, memorizing abstract patterns, and hoping your brain makes the connection between what you see on paper and what you hear. Most people give up before they ever experience the joy of understanding how music actually works.

For guitarists specifically, the challenge is even greater:

- Guitar fretboard patterns are confusing and inconsistent across strings

SMGuitar eliminates these barriers by making music theory visual, interactive, and immediately audible.

### How It Works: A Journey Through the App

#### Step 1: Choose Your Musical Key

The moment you open SMGuitar, you're greeted by our signature **Chromatic Wheel**—a beautiful, interactive circle displaying all 12 musical notes. Simply rotate the wheel with your finger to select any note as your musical home base (the "tonic" or "root note").

The wheel is elegant in its simplicity: spin it, watch the notes glide smoothly into position, and see your selection snap perfectly into place. There's something deeply satisfying about this interaction—it feels like tuning into a radio station, but for musical keys.

#### Step 2: Select Your Scale and Mode

Once you've chosen your root note, use the intuitive dropdown menus to select from dozens of musical scales:

- **Major Scale** - The happy, bright sound of pop music
- **Natural Minor** - The emotional, introspective sound
- **Pentatonic Scales** - The universally appealing 5-note scales used in rock, blues, and folk
- **Blues Scale** - Add that soulful, gritty character
- **Modes** - Explore Dorian, Mixolydian, Phrygian, and more for sophisticated sounds
- **Harmonic & Melodic Minor** - Classical and jazz flavors
- **And many more...**

Each scale has multiple **modes** (variations that start on different notes), giving you access to hundreds of unique musical flavors.

#### Step 3: Visualize on Multiple Instruments

Here's where SMGuitar truly shines. Your selected scale is simultaneously displayed on:

**The Chromatic Wheel**
The wheel updates to show which notes belong to your scale, with each note color-coded by its musical function. The root note stands out immediately, and you can see at a glance how the scale spans the full chromatic spectrum.

**The Piano Keyboard**
A beautiful, scrollable 6-octave piano appears below the wheel. Scale notes are highlighted in elegant blue, while the root note glows orange. Tap any key to hear it play—the sound is crisp, clear, and immediate.

This piano visualization is incredibly valuable because piano is the universal language of music theory. Even if you've never touched a piano, seeing scales laid out on the keys helps you understand intervals and patterns that transfer to any instrument.

**The Guitar Fretboard**
Navigate to the fretboard view to see your scale mapped across the guitar neck. Each note displays either its name or scale degree (your choice), with Roman numerals marking fret positions. The fretboard painter shows exactly where to place your fingers.

For chord fingerings, SMGuitar includes a library of pre-computed fingering positions, so you're not just seeing "this note exists here"—you're seeing "put your fingers exactly here to play this chord."

#### Step 4: Build Your Chord Progression

This is the magic moment. Based on your selected scale, SMGuitar generates the **diatonic chords**—the chords that naturally belong to your key.

Each chord appears as a colorful button:

- **I (Tonic)** - Green - Home base, resolution
- **ii** - Lemon Yellow - Subtle motion
- **iii** - Orange - Emotional color
- **IV (Subdominant)** - Blue-Green - Pre-chorus energy
- **V (Dominant)** - Yellow-Green - Tension, wants to resolve
- **vi** - Yellow - The relative minor, emotional depth
- **vii°** - Red - Maximum tension, diminished

These aren't arbitrary colors—they're a visual language that helps you understand chord function. After using SMGuitar for a while, you'll start to think in colors: "I need some green for resolution, then some yellow-green to create tension."

**Tap a chord** to add it to your progression (2 beats).
**Double-tap a chord** to add it with longer duration (4 beats).

Watch as your progression builds in the visual bar at the bottom—a timeline of colored blocks representing your musical journey.

#### Step 5: Hit Play and Listen

This is the moment everything comes together.

Press the play button and your chord progression comes to life with:

- **Piano/Keys** - Playing lush chord voicings
- **Bass** - Grounding your progression with deep tones
- **Drums** - Keeping the rhythm steady

The audio quality is exceptional because SMGuitar uses professional-grade **SoundFont** technology—the same synthesis method used in commercial music production software. The FluidR3_GM SoundFont provides over 100MB of sampled instrument sounds, ensuring every note sounds authentic.

You can customize each instrument:

**Keyboard Sounds:**
- Grand Piano (classic, versatile)
- Rhodes (warm, electric piano tone)
- Organ (sustained, harmonic-rich)
- Pad (ambient, atmospheric)
- Synthesizer (modern, electronic)

**Bass Sounds:**
- Double Bass (acoustic, orchestral)
- Electric Bass (punchy, modern)
- Synth Bass (electronic, powerful)

**Drum Kits:**
- Acoustic Kit (natural, organic)
- Electronic Kit (crisp, modern)

#### Step 6: Control Your Playback

The player interface gives you complete control:

- **Play/Stop** - Instant response, no lag
- **Loop** - Repeat your progression endlessly for practice
- **Tempo** - Adjust from slow practice speeds to high-energy tempos (40-200 BPM)
- **Metronome** - Toggle an audio click for timing practice
- **Volume per Track** - Mix your instruments perfectly

The visual beat indicator shows you exactly where you are in the progression, highlighting the current beat as it plays. This is invaluable for practice—you can see and hear the rhythm simultaneously.

#### Step 7: Save and Build a Library

Love what you created? Save your progression to your personal library. Give it a name, come back to it later, and build a collection of your best ideas.

This is perfect for:
- Songwriters capturing ideas on the go
- Teachers building lesson examples
- Students creating practice materials
- Anyone who wants to remember "that cool chord thing I figured out"

### The Technology Behind the Magic

SMGuitar isn't a simple app—it's built on professional-grade technology:

**Audio Engine**
The app uses a custom MIDI sequencer engine that schedules audio events with millisecond precision. This means notes play exactly when they should, with no noticeable delay between your tap and the sound.

**SoundFont Synthesis**
Rather than using simple synthesized tones, SMGuitar uses a 100MB+ SoundFont library containing samples of real instruments. Every piano note, bass pluck, and drum hit is a recording of an actual instrument, not a computer-generated approximation.

**Real-Time State Management**
The app maintains complex state across multiple dimensions: your selected key, scale, mode, chord progression, instrument settings, playback position, and more. Everything stays in sync, updating instantly as you make changes.

**Cross-Platform Excellence**
Built with Flutter, SMGuitar provides a native experience on both iOS and Android. The code is optimized for each platform, ensuring smooth animations and responsive audio on whatever device you use.

```jsx
        backgroundColor: colors.backgroundDark,
        position: 'relative',
        transform: `rotate(${rotation}deg)`,
      }}
    >
      {NOTES.map((note, index) => {
        const angle = (index * 30 - 90) * (Math.PI / 180);
        const x = radius + noteRadius * Math.cos(angle) - 15;
        const y = radius + noteRadius * Math.sin(angle) - 15;
        const isActive = activeNotes.includes(note);

        return (
          <div
            key={note}
            style={{
              position: 'absolute',
              left: x,
              top: y,
              width: 30,
              height: 30,
              borderRadius: '50%',
              backgroundColor: isActive ? colors.accent : colors.surface,
              display: 'flex',
              alignItems: 'center',
              justifyContent: 'center',
              color: colors.textPrimary,
              fontSize: 12,
              fontWeight: 600,
              transform: `rotate(${-rotation}deg)`,
            }}
          >
            {note}
          </div>
        );
      })}

      {/* Center circle */}
      <div
        style={{
          position: 'absolute',
          left: '50%',
          top: '50%',
          transform: 'translate(-50%, -50%)',
          width: radius * 0.5,
          height: radius * 0.5,
          borderRadius: '50%',
          backgroundColor: colors.backgroundDark,
          border: `2px solid ${colors.border}`,
          display: 'flex',
          alignItems: 'center',
          justifyContent: 'center',
          color: colors.accent,
          fontSize: 24,
          fontWeight: 700,
        }}
      >
        {NOTES[Math.round(-rotation / 30) % 12] || 'C'}
      </div>
    </div>
  );
};
```

```jsx
// src/components/ChordButton.jsx
import { spring, useCurrentFrame } from 'remotion';
import { scaleDegreeColors } from '../theme';

export const ChordButton = ({
  chordName,
  degree,
```

### Progression Bar Component

```jsx
// src/components/ProgressionBar.jsx
import { interpolate, useCurrentFrame } from 'remotion';
import { colors, scaleDegreeColors } from '../theme';

export const ProgressionBar = ({
  chords,
  currentBeat = 0,
  totalBeats = 16,
  isPlaying = false
}) => {
  const frame = useCurrentFrame();

  const playheadPosition = interpolate(
    currentBeat,
    [0, totalBeats],
    [0, 100],
    { extrapolateRight: 'clamp' }
  );

  return (
    <div
      style={{
        height: 48,
        backgroundColor: colors.surface,
        borderRadius: 12,
        border: `1px solid ${colors.border}`,
        padding: 8,
        display: 'flex',
        gap: 4,
        position: 'relative',
        overflow: 'hidden',
      }}
    >
      {chords.map((chord, index) => (
        <div
          key={index}
          style={{
            flex: chord.duration,
            height: '100%',
            borderRadius: 8,
            backgroundColor: scaleDegreeColors[chord.degree],
            display: 'flex',
            alignItems: 'center',
            justifyContent: 'center',
            color: '#FFFFFF',
            fontSize: 12,
            fontWeight: 600,
          }}
        >
          {chord.name}
        </div>
      ))}

      {/* Playhead */}
      {isPlaying && (
        <div
          style={{
            position: 'absolute',
            left: `${playheadPosition}%`,
            top: 0,
            width: 2,
            height: '100%',
            backgroundColor: colors.accent,
            transition: 'left 0.1s linear',
          }}
        />
      )}
    </div>
  );
};
```
